The mythical story of fallen angels preserved in 1 Enoch and related literature was profoundly influential during the Second Temple period. In The Myth of Rebellious Angels renowned scholar, Loren Stuckenbruck explores aspects of that influence and demonstrates how the myth was reused and adapted to address new religious and cultural contexts.
For Second Temple Jewish authors, the pericope that perhaps most inspired speculation about the “fallen angels” myth is the enigmatic passage of Genesis 6:1–4.
